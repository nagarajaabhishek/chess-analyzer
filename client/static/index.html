About ChessNow

The Screen-Free, Voice-Controlled Chess Hotline

♟️ Why Voice Chess?

Traditional digital chess interfaces rely on screens, touch coordinates, and precise mouse movements. ChessNow shifts the game to the telephone network. By dialing +1-(4-CHESSNOW-3), you can play complete games entirely hands-free and screen-free from any phone—whether it's a landline, a flip phone, or a modern smartphone.

🤖 Natural Speech vs. "NATO Code"

Most voice-controlled applications fail because speech recognizers struggle to differentiate coordinate letters (like mistaking "D4" for "E4" or "B4"), forcing players to speak in military code ("Echo 4", "Delta 5"). ChessNow's AI companion, Thara, is built for natural dialogue. Speak normally—say "e4", "Knight to f3", or "takes takes". If a move is ambiguous or heard with low confidence, Thara will conversationally ask you to confirm: "Did you mean Knight to c3?"

🧠 Progressive Blindfold & Visualization Training

For sighted players looking to improve calculation, spatial memory, and board visualization, screen-free chess is the ultimate training tool. ChessNow lets you practice blindfold play incrementally. Start by keeping this web dashboard open as a visual cue, progress to peeking only when lost, and eventually transition to pure auditory play, using voice commands like "repeat position" or "what's on e4" to check your mental board state.

♿ Screen-Free Accessibility

For visually impaired, blind, or motor-disabled players, screen readers and grid keyboard shortcuts are often clumsy and exhausting. ChessNow provides a barrier-free, plug-and-play hotline. The telephony line and natural voice loop bypass complex assistive tech interfaces, making chess accessible to everyone.

🚶 Built for Multitasking

Play chess while walking, riding the train, exercising, cooking dinner, or resting your eyes. Plug in your headset, dial the hotline, and immerse yourself in the game without being tethered to a keyboard or screen.
